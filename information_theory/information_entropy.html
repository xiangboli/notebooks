Example:

One flip of a fair coin

Before the flip, there are two equally probable choices: heads or tails. After the flip, we’ve narrowed it down to one choice. Amount of information = log2(2/1) = 1 bit.

Roll of two dice

Each die has six faces, so in the roll of two dice there are 36 possible combinations for the outcome. Amount of information = log2(36/1) = 5.2 bits.
